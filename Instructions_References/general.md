Instructions from MEEN RPS:

    For the 305 classes, we are giving them 25 credits to use for their projects. They will need to come in to get trained on FDM**print**ing if they haven't already, which is free and linked below in our reservation calendar. Most of them should already be trained from MEEN 210. One hour of **print**ing is 1.25 credits, so 25 should be way more than enough for the students to work with. Every student also gets 15 credits per semester. Their designated "team lead" will receive the credits during their 3D **print**ing training, or if they've already been trained, they can send us an email at [meen3dprinting@tamu.edu](mailto:meen3dprinting@tamu.edu) or come ask in person. We will be keeping track of the groups and if they've received credits or not.

    As a general rule here at the RPS, we would advise that students use **PLA** only. There are other filaments that we stock that are stronger, but they are much harder to **print** with and more expensive. If students want to get into the material selection, we will see if we can make that happen, but we would likely have to decrease the credit allotment. The other polymer options are ASA, TPU, and PETG.

    In terms of general advice when it comes to the **print**ing process, I would advise students to think about layer lines while **print**ing. Layer lines are the border in between layers as the part is being **print**ed, which is caused by hot **pla**stic being deposited onto cooled **pla**stic. This is the single biggest failure point of 3D **print**s under load. If the cross section of the beam is along these lines, the part will almost certainly fail under significant load. If the layer lines are along the span of the beam, it will be able to withstand much higher loads. Also, the infill of the part can affect strength, which is the density of the internal structure. Both of these parameters are controlled within the slicer after exporting the .stl file from SolidWorks. If they need any help with slicing or **print**ing, they can always come see us at JCAIN 422, or visit our wiki linked below. I hope this helped and let us know if you have any other questions.
